# 📈 Paisa Metrics & Trading Guide

This document provides a simple reference for the core metrics used on **paisa.example.com** to help you make data-driven trading decisions.

---

## 1. The Paisa Score (0-100)
**What it is:** A "Grade" for a stock's overall quality and growth potential.
- **Formula:** A weighted average of fundamental health, technical momentum, and AI insights.
- **Why it’s useful:** It instantly tells you if a stock is an **Elite** pick (Score > 90) or a **Speculative** gamble (Score < 50).
- **Trading Action:** 
    - **90+**: Look for aggressive strategies (ZEBRA / LEAPS).
    - **75-89**: Look for income strategies (Cash Secured Puts).

## 2. Beta-Weighted Exposure
**What it is:** Your total "Market Risk" expressed in S&P 500 (SPY) terms.
- **Formula:** `(Quantity × Price × Beta) / SPY Price`
- **Why it’s useful:** It lets you know if you are accidentally "too long" or "too short" the market. 
- **Example:** If your exposure is $500,000, your portfolio will move almost exactly like $500,000 worth of SPY shares, regardless of what individual stocks you own.

## 3. Portfolio Health Score
**What it is:** A 0-100 rating of how well-balanced your entire account is.
- **Formula:** Combined score of **Diversification** (Sectors), **Concentration** (Top holdings), and **Correlation** (How much your stocks move together).
- **Why it’s useful:** A low score (< 50) warns you that your portfolio is "Fragile"—meaning a single bad headline or sector drop could wipe out your gains.

## 4. Expected Move
**What it is:** The predicted "Price Box" the stock is expected to stay within over a specific timeframe.
- **Formula:** `Stock Price × Implied Volatility × √ (Days / 365)`
- **Why it’s useful:** It tells you where to set your stop-losses and take-profit targets. 
- **Trading Action:** If you are selling Puts, you want your "Strike Price" to be **outside** the Expected Move range for a higher probability of success.

## 5. Relative Strength (RS)
**What it is:** A measure of how much a stock is beating the S&P 500.
- **Formula:** `Stock % Change - Benchmark % Change` (calculated over 20 and 60 days).
- **Why it’s useful:** Professional traders look for "Green" RS. If the market is down -2% but your stock is only down -0.5%, it has **Positive Relative Strength** and is likely to rocket higher when the market recovers.

## 6. Altman Z-Score
**What it is:** A "Bankruptcy Predictor."
- **Threshold:** > 3.0 is Safe; < 1.8 is in the "Distress Zone."
- **Why it’s useful:** It prevents you from "Catching a falling knife" on a company that might go out of business.

## 7. IV Rank (Implied Volatility Rank)
**What it is:** Tells you if option prices are currently "Expensive" or "Cheap" relative to their history.
- **Trading Action:** 
    - **High IV Rank (> 50)**: Sell premium (get paid more for taking risk).
    - **Low IV Rank (< 25)**: Buy options (they are "on sale").

## 8. Stress Projection (Stress Test)
**What it is:** A "What-If" scenario that simulates a sharp market crash.
- **The Scenario:** Assumes a **-10% static drop** in the S&P 500 (SPY) and a **50% expansion** in Option Implied Volatility (IV).
- **Components:**
    - **Projected Dollar Impact**: The estimated total $ amount your account would lose in this crash.
    - **Drawdown %**: The percentage of your total account value that would be wiped out.
    - **VIX Impact**: The specific $ impact caused by the "IV Spike." For Long LEAPS, this is often positive (cushioning the fall), but for Short Options, this increases the loss significantly.
- **Why it’s useful:** It helps you understand if you are "Over-Leveraged." If your Projected Drawdown is > 30% for a -10% market move, your account is likely too aggressive.
